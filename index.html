About


Understanding how environmental factors, such as temperature, affect the strength of species interactions and, in turn, the dynamics of ecological communities is a major challenge for ecologists, particularly in the face of climate change. While the direct, physiological effects of extrinsic environmental factors on individuals and populations are generally well understood, much less is known about how these effects propagate through food webs via networks of species interactions and alter community structure and dynamics. Predicting the higher-order impacts of environmental variability on community dynamics is the overarching goal of my research.


I am a community ecologist broadly trained in field- and lab-based aquatic ecology techniques as well as statistical and mathematical modeling, making me well equipped to ask and address theory-driven empirical research questions. I am currently a postdoc in Mark Novak's Lab working on how allometry affects predictive certainty in complex ecological networks.
